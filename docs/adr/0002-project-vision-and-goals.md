# ADR 0002: Project Vision and Goals

**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

Every previous iteration of this site failed for the same reason: it didn't
create a habit. The blog lost momentum. The second brain had too much workflow
friction to maintain. The vcard has no reason to revisit at all.

The next version needs to break this cycle.

## Decision

The site should serve four purposes:

1. **Hassle-free blogging** - Writing and publishing should have minimal
   friction. If it's annoying to publish, it won't get published.

2. **Resume** - Make professional experience available to anyone who wants it.

3. **Virtual business card** - A starting point for people to discover
   presence on other platforms and ways to get in touch.

4. **Something worth coming back to** - The site itself should be engaging
   enough to tinker with, refine, and evolve over time. It should pull its
   owner back in, not push them away with maintenance burden.

## Consequences

- The blogging workflow is a first-class concern, not an afterthought. Whatever
  tech choices are made, ease of publishing is a hard requirement.
- The site needs enough surface area to be interesting to work on, but not so
  much that it becomes a chore.
- Resume and contact/links content should be straightforward to keep current.
- Tech stack decisions (ADR TBD) should be evaluated against these goals,
  particularly the friction and engagement criteria.
